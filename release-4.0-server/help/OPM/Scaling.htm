6. Scaling Mechanisms 

Scaling is the process of changing the level of detail. Scaling is generalization of Elaboration  and Simplification . Those opposing purposes are carried out by Refining (Elaboration) and Abstracting  (Simplification). The purpose of Refining is to provide more details about one or more things in the system. The purpose of Abstracting is to obtain simpler, clearer, more readable diagrams. Logically, Refining and Abstracting are inverses of each other.
There are two primary scaling mechanisms in OPM: Zooming-in/Zooming-out and Unfolding/Folding.
