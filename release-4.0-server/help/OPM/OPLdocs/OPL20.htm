OPL 2.0 Sentences:

Structural Relations:

(1)   Relation sentence:

a.      Bank relates to Account.

b.     Jacob and Rachel are married.

c.      Firm employs 45 to 60 Programmers and 75 to 100 Salespersons.

(2)   Aggregation sentence:

a.      Consortium consists of Bank.

b.     Consortium consists of 5 Banks.

(3)   State enumeration sentence:

a.      Size can be unary, binary or multi-valued.

b.     Person is initially baby and ultimately elder.

c.      Person is healthy by default.

d.     Value of Temperature is 80.

e.      Value of Salary is equal to or greater than 1200 and equal to or less than 7800.

(4)   Exhibition sentence:

a.      Exhibitor exhibits Feature.

b.     Attribute exhibits Size, which can be unary, binary or multi-valued, Mode, which can be quantitative or qualitative, and Touch, which can be hard or soft.

c.      Factory exhibits Manufacturing.

d.     Employee exhibits Salary and Performance, as well as Performing.

(5)   Physical thing sentence:

a.      Factory is a physical object.

b.     Manufacturing is a physical process.

c.      Operator, Machine, and Raw Material are physical objects.

(6)   Specialization sentence:

a.      Ford is a Car.

b.     Pilot, Sailor, and Driver are Occupations.

c.      Boiling, Frying, and Grilling are Cooking.

d.     Exposed Film and digitized Diskette are recorded Capturing Media.

(7)   Instantiation sentence:

a.      Lassie is an instance of Dog.

b.     Empire State Building Object is an instance of Building Object, the Height of which is Tall.

 

Procedural Links:

(1)   Agent sentence:

a. Customer handles Transaction Executing.

b.Pilot, Sailor, and Driver handle Flying, Sailing, and Driving, respectively.

c. 2 to 4 Mechanics and an optional Engineer handle Blade Replacing

(2)   Instrument sentence:

a. Manufacturing requires Model and Machine.

b.Rendering requires either Photo Shop or Computer.

c. Frying, Grilling, and Boiling require Pan, Grill, and Pot, respectively.

d.Blade Replacing uses optional Machine Tools.

(3)   Effect sentence:

a. Transaction Executing affects Account and Cash.

b.Salary Updating affects the value of Salary.

c. Lighting changes Lamp from on to off.

(4)   XOR effect-instrument sentence:

a. Rendering either affects Film or requires Diskette.

(5)   Result sentence:

a. Manufacturing yields Product and Production Report.

b.Transaction Executing yields either Transaction or Denial Notice.

c. Either Notifying or Transaction Processing yields Denial Notice. (xor meaning)

d.Notifying or Transaction Processing yields Denial Notice. (or meaning)

e. Cash Card Validating determines whether Cash Card is valid

f.   Approval Denying yields denied Approval.

(6)   Consumption sentence:

a. Blade Replacing consumes at least one Blade.

b.Exposing consumes blank Capturing Medium.

(7)   Result-consumption sentence:

a. Manufacturing consumes approved Raw Material and yields manufactured Product.

(8)   Condition sentence:

a. Transaction Processing occurs if Approval is obtained.

b.Approval Denying occurs if Cash Card is not valid.

c. Password Checking occurs if Cash Card is valid, otherwise Approval Denying occurs.

(9)   Agent condition sentence:

a. User must be authorized for Accessing to occur.

(10)       Invocation sentence:

a. Exposing invokes Recording.

 

Diagrams Relations:

a. Transaction Executing from SD zooms in SD1 into Account Checking, Transaction Processing, and Notifying, as well as Approval and Card Data. 

(2)   Out-zooming sentence:

a. Cash Card Validating, Password Checking, and Approval Denying, as well as Cash Card is valid? from SD1.1 zoom out as Account Checking in SD1.

a. Root from SD1 unfolds in SD1.1 to expose the parts Part A, Part B, and Part C.

e. Rooting from SD6 unfolds in SD6.1 to expose the operation Operating, as well as the attributes Attr A and Attr B.

f.   Root from SD1 unfolds in SD1.1 to expose the parts Part A, Part B, and Part C and the attributes Attr A and Attr B, as well as the operation Operating.
