6. Scaling Mechanisms 

6.1 In-Zooming/Out-Zooming

Zooming into an abstract thing decreases the distance of viewing it such that lower-level things enclosed within the thing become visible.  Conversely, zooming out of a refined thing increases the distance of viewing it, such that a set of lower-level things that are enclosed within it become invisible. 

Transaction Executing is zoomed into its sub-processes and local variables.

Transaction Executing is zoomed out.
