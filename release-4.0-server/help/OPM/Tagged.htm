4. Structural Relations 

    4.1 General Strcutural Relations

A structural relation is a connection or an association between things holds irrespective of time. Structural relations are symbolized by arrows. An optional meaningful tag is usually written along the structural relations, allowing countless variations.
 

The logical Client represents the physical, enivronmental Customer.

Each end of a structural relation may have a multuplicity constraint, denoting how many instances of one end can be connected to one instance of the second relation end. The defauly multuplicity constraint is 1.

There are four special types of  structural relations in OPM:

  • Aggregation - Participation

  • Generalization - Specialization

  • Featuring - Characterization

  • Classification - Instantiation
